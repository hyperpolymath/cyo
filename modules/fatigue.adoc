= Route: The Long Quest (Fatigue & Fragmentation)

[.lead]
_You do not actually hate any one tool. You are tired of being told that
growing means starting over._

This is the meta-route, and the honest one. The exhaustion is not "this linker
is slow." It is the recurring demand that to gain something — safety, proof,
speed — you abandon your dialect, your libraries, and the guild you built them
with. That demand is the real boss on this map.

== The small wards, named honestly

* *Transpilers* (Babel, esbuild) buy you new features on old runtimes. Relief,
  not a cure — the fragmentation is still there underneath.
* *Supersets and typed siblings* (TypeScript, and the roads in `scripts`)
  improve safety but keep you bound to one ecosystem's fate.
* *A universal target* (WebAssembly) lets many languages run anywhere — but you
  still pick one language to *write*, and inherit its world.

These are listed plainly so the map does not oversell. Each is a real ward; none
ends the quest.

== The route that addresses the boss directly: the Federation

The fatigue comes from coupling **identity** (the dialect and community you love)
to **origin** (the guarantees your code can offer). Separate the two and the
boss loses its grip:

* Your community keeps its *face* — the dialect it already speaks and evolves.
* Every face lowers to one shared core of **affine and dependent types**
  targeting **typed-WASM** — see
  https://github.com/hyperpolymath/affinescriptiser[AffineScript].
* "Affine" = a resource used *at most once*, tracked by the type system. You
  invest your logic once and it stays interoperable across every face.

What you can now survive: the next "must-learn" cycle, without throwing away
your expertise or your guild to take part in it. You grow without emigrating.

Cost, stated plainly: it is a younger ecosystem; you arrive as an early citizen.
The trade is deliberate — you spend some maturity-of-tooling to stop spending
your identity.

== This route opens onto

* `scripts` — the Federation in concrete, runnable terms.
* `logic` — wizardry, if the part worth preserving forever is the *reasoning*.
* `git` — the Chronicler, when the fatigue is really process, not language.

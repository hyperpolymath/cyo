== Why this exists

Most tooling advice is written as a deficit list: your stack is "infamous,"
"notoriously complex," a "fragmented mess," and here is the ladder out. That
framing is both wrong and counterproductive. It is wrong because you got real
work done with that stack. It is counterproductive because nobody adopts a tool
they were shamed into.

`cyo` starts from the opposite premise: you *enjoy* writing code. Given that,
the interesting question is not "what is wrong with you" but "what adventure do
you want next, and what do you want your code to be able to survive afterwards."

== Routes, not levels

There are deliberately no numbered levels. A numbered ladder encodes a
hierarchy — rung one is the beginner's shame, rung four is the enlightened — and
that hierarchy is exactly what alienates working developers from otherwise good
advice.

A route is a lateral choice. Each one is a peer of the others. Each reflects an
aspect of identity:

* the part of you that wants speed and a tight feedback loop,
* the part that wants proof and an unbroken oath,
* the part that wants to keep your community and your dialect,
* the part that just wants to learn something with wonder in it.

You pick the route that fits the player you are this season. Next season you may
pick another. Nothing is foreclosed.

== Battle-ready as the destination

Every chapter is honest about one thing: *what can your code now survive that it
could not before?* Production load. A hostile input. A decade of maintenance. A
safety regulator. A teammate who does not know your invariants.

"Battle-ready" is not a vibe. It is a concrete property gained, stated plainly,
alongside the concrete cost and how hard the route is to walk back. An adventure
with no stated stakes is a sales pitch, not a choice.

== A note on the Federation

Several routes lead toward
https://github.com/hyperpolymath/affinescriptiser[AffineScript]. The idea is
simple and is described without metaphor here so it cannot be misread:

* You keep writing in the dialect your community already speaks — your *face*.
* That dialect lowers to a shared, stable core of affine and dependent types.
* The core targets typed-WASM, so components written in different faces
  interoperate with resource guarantees intact.

"Affine" means a resource is used *at most once*; the type system tracks that.
The point is emancipation, not conversion: you grow your code's guarantees
without abandoning the people you build with. That is the whole of it. No
states, no republics, no allegory.

. No claim is made that cannot be checked.
. Every route states its cost and its reversibility.
. You enjoy this. The map respects that you enjoy this.
